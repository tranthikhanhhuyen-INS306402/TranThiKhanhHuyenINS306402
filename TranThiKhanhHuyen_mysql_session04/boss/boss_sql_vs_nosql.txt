Boss Fight: SQL vs NoSQL Decision

## Scenario 1: High-Velocity Social Media Feed

**Choice: NoSQL (e.g., document or wide-column database)**

### Justification:

1. **Horizontal Scalability (Critical for High Velocity)**

   * Social media feeds generate massive write and read traffic.
   * NoSQL databases are designed for **horizontal scaling (scale-out)** across distributed nodes.
   * Adding more servers handles spikes better than vertical scaling alone.

2. **Schema Flexibility**

   * Social posts may contain text, images, videos, hashtags, reactions, comments, and evolving metadata.
   * NoSQL supports **flexible / dynamic schema**, allowing new fields without migrations.
   * This is ideal for fast product iteration.

3. **Performance over Strict ACID**

   * Social feeds prioritize availability and speed.
   * Eventual consistency (BASE model) is acceptable (e.g., a like count being briefly outdated is fine).
   * Full ACID compliance is not strictly required for feed display.

4. **High Write Throughput**

   * NoSQL systems are optimized for high insert/update rates.
   * Better suited for time-series and feed-style workloads.

**Conclusion:**
For a high-velocity social media feed, NoSQL provides better horizontal scalability, flexible schema handling, and performance under heavy distributed traffic.

## Scenario 2: Secure Bank Ledger

**Choice: SQL (Relational Database)**

### Justification:

1. **ACID Compliance (Non-Negotiable)**

   * Banking systems require strict **ACID properties**:

     * Atomicity (transaction fully completes or fails)
     * Consistency (no invalid states)
     * Isolation (no dirty reads)
     * Durability (no data loss after commit)
   * Financial transactions cannot tolerate eventual consistency.

2. **Strong Data Integrity & Constraints**

   * SQL databases enforce:

     * Foreign keys
     * Constraints
     * Referential integrity
   * Prevents inconsistent balances or orphaned transactions.

3. **Structured Schema**

   * Bank ledgers have well-defined entities: accounts, transactions, balances.
   * A fixed schema ensures predictable structure and auditing.
   * Schema rigidity improves compliance and regulatory reporting.

4. **Transactional Reliability Over Horizontal Scaling**

   * Banks prioritize correctness over infinite scalability.
   * Vertical scaling with replication is typically sufficient.
   * Strong consistency is more important than flexible distribution.

**Conclusion:**
For a secure bank ledger, SQL is the correct choice because it guarantees ACID compliance, enforces strict integrity constraints, and ensures transactional correctness.

# Why It Matters

Choosing the wrong database model can cause:

* Data inconsistency
* System downtime under scale
* Regulatory violations
* Financial loss

Engineers must evaluate:

* ACID requirements
* Scalability model (vertical vs horizontal)
* Schema flexibility needs
* Consistency guarantees

The database model must match the business risk profile and workload characteristics.
